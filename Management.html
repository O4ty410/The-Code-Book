Finance & Budgeting

Understanding P&L (Profit & Loss)

The foundation of financial management

📊 What is a P&L?

A P&L statement shows whether your business made money or lost money over a period (usually a month, quarter, or year).

Simple formula: Revenue − Expenses = Profit (or Loss)
🎯 The Three Parts

1. Revenue (Money In)
All the money your business earned. For a hotel: room sales, food & beverage, events, etc.

2. Expenses (Money Out)
Wages, rent, utilities, stock, cleaning supplies, insurance, etc.

3. Profit/Loss (The Result)
What's left after you subtract expenses from revenue.

💡 Real Example
Monthly P&L - Hotel Restaurant
Revenue: £10,000 Less: Staff wages: £-3,500 Food costs: £-2,000 Utilities: £-800 Cleaning: £-400 ------------------- Profit: £3,300
🔍 Why This Matters as a Manager
1. You control costs (wages, waste, stock). Better management = higher profit.
2. You drive revenue (customer satisfaction, upselling, retention).
3. Your boss measures you on it. Understanding the P&L means understanding what you're responsible for.

People Management

Building Trust with Your Team

The foundation of effective leadership

🤝 Why Trust Matters

Trust is the difference between a team that works FOR you and a team that works WITH you. Without trust, you spend energy managing instead of leading.

5 Ways to Build Trust (Fast)
1. Be consistent — Do what you say you'll do. Every time. Small promises matter.
2. Be visible and present — Be on the floor, not hiding in the office.
3. Admit mistakes — When you get something wrong, own it.
4. Give credit, take responsibility — When things go well, credit your team. When things go wrong, you take it.
5. Listen before you fix — Ask questions. Understand their perspective. THEN give direction.
💬 The Trust Conversation
Trust-Building Conversation Script
1. "I've noticed X [specific thing]" 2. "Help me understand what's going on?" 3. [Listen. Don't interrupt.] 4. "Here's what I'm seeing / concerned about" 5. "What do you need from me?" 6. "Here's what I need from you" 7. "Let's check in on Friday"
🚩 Trust Killers (Avoid These)
Making promises you don't keep — Even small ones ("I'll follow up" then you don't).
Blaming your team publicly — Never. Always address issues privately first.
Inconsistent rules — If one person gets away with something, everyone notices.
Not following through — If they raise an issue and you ignore it, they stop raising issues.
Scenario: What would you do?

A team member misses a deadline. Your customer is upset. Do you:

Tell the customer it was their fault
Pull them aside privately, understand why, then handle the customer
Ignore it and hope it doesn't happen again
Recruitment

Hiring the Right Person

The most important management decision

⭐ Why Hiring Well Matters

A bad hire costs you 2–3x their annual salary in lost productivity, management time, and replacement costs.

A great hire saves you constantly. They work with you, not against you.

🎯 Before You Post the Job
Job Spec Checklist
[ ] What will they DO? (daily/weekly tasks) [ ] What RESULTS do they need to deliver? [ ] What skills are essential? (must-have) [ ] What skills are nice-to-have? [ ] What's their reporting structure? [ ] What problems will they solve? [ ] What's the salary/benefits?
🔍 Interview Red Flags & Green Flags
Red flag: Blames everyone else for past problems.
Green flag: Takes ownership ("I'd do that differently now").
Red flag: Doesn't ask you questions about the role or team.
Green flag: Asks smart questions showing they've thought about the role.
Red flag: Vague answers. Can't give specific examples.
Green flag: Clear, specific examples with context and outcomes.
✓ UK Employment Law (Basics)
You can't ask about: Age, health, family plans, marital status, religion, ethnicity, sexual orientation.
You CAN ask about: Ability to do the job, relevant experience, availability, references.
Probation: UK employees have employment rights from day 1, not after 3 months.

Finance & Budgeting

Building and Managing a Budget

How to forecast and control spend

📋 What is a Budget?

A budget is a forecast of what you expect to happen with money over a period (usually a year, broken into months).

It's your management tool: "This is what we planned. Are we on track? If not, why not?"

🎯 Basic Budget Steps
1. Start with history — Look at last year's actual spend.
2. Build from zero where it matters — Wages, rent, supplies based on activity.
3. Add contingency — Things always cost more. Add 5–10%.
4. Review quarterly — Compare actual to budget. Understand the gaps.
💰 Budget Template
Monthly Budget Example
REVENUE Sales: £12,000 ------------------- Total Revenue: £12,000 EXPENSES Staff: £4,000 Stock/Supplies: £2,400 Utilities: £600 Equipment: £300 Contingency (5%): £350 ------------------- Total Expenses: £7,650 PROFIT: £4,350
🔴 Budget Variance

Scenario: You budgeted £2,000 for stock but spent £2,400. Ask why:

Unexpected waste or damage?
More customers than forecast?
Prices increased?
Poor ordering discipline?

A 20% variance from demand is very different from 20% because of waste.

💡 Your Job as a Manager
Control what you control: Wages, waste, stock, overtime.
Report honestly: Flag overspend early. Surprises are worse than bad news.
Find the leaks: £50/week waste in stock = £2,600/year.
People Management

Feedback and Performance Conversations

How to give feedback that sticks

🎯 Why Feedback Matters

Most people want to do well. But if they don't know what "well" looks like, they can't improve. Your job is to be clear and give feedback quickly.

⭐ Positive Feedback (Do This More)
Good Feedback Example
"That's exactly what I needed. You spotted the customer issue before it escalated and handled it calmly. That's the standard I'm looking for."

NOT: "Good job!" (vague, forgettable)

🚨 Corrective Feedback Script
Corrective Feedback
1. Private conversation (not in front of others) 2. "I noticed you did X when Y happened" 3. "The impact was Z" (be specific) 4. "Here's what I need instead" 5. "Do you understand?" 6. "Let's check in Friday"
🚫 Feedback Mistakes
Don't make it personal: "You're disorganised" vs "That report had errors in three rows".
Don't surprise them at review time: Tell them NOW, not in 3 months.
Don't give feedback when angry: Wait 30 mins. Be professional.
Don't forget the follow-up: If you said "check in Friday", DO IT.
💬 Development Conversation (Quarterly)
Growth Talk
1. "What's going well for you right now?" 2. "What would you like to get better at?" 3. "What do you need from me to do that?" 4. "Here's what I see as your strengths / growth areas" 5. "Here's how we can work on that together"
Recruitment

Onboarding: Setting New Hires Up for Success

The first 30 days matter most

⏰ The First 30 Days Matter

People decide if they're staying within the first 30 days. A bad onboarding costs you. A great one means they're productive faster and more likely to stay.

📅 Day 1 Checklist
First Day Must-Haves
[ ] Welcome them personally [ ] Show them the physical space [ ] Give key info (hours, breaks, contacts, safety) [ ] Introduce 3-4 team members [ ] Give them ONE clear task [ ] Check in at end of day (5 mins)
📋 Week 1 — Get Them Stable
Shadow an experienced team member — They see how work actually gets done.
Cover the basics — Procedures, systems, key rules. Don't assume they know.
Give small wins — Build confidence. Not big complex tasks yet.
Daily 5-min check-in — "How's it going? Any questions?"
🚀 30-Day Check-In
30-Day Review Meeting
1. "How's the first month been?" 2. "What's felt manageable? What's been hard?" 3. "Here's what I'm seeing going well: [specific]" 4. "Here's what we need to work on: [specific]" 5. "What do you need from me?" 6. "Here's the plan for month 2" 7. Be clear: "You're on track" or "needs improvement"
🚩 Red Flags During Onboarding
Not asking questions — Ask: "What are you unsure about?"
Not showing up on time — Address day 3, not day 15.
Repeating mistakes — Try a different approach or re-pair them.
